About Me

"Nerver forget where you came from"

I am actively looking for a research internship in high-performance system design in Fall 2019!

My name is Kai Wu (吴凯). I am a Computer Science PhD student in EECS at University of California, Merced, where I am a member of Parallel Architecture, System, and Algorithm Lab (PASA)and advised by Dr. Dong Li. Before coming to UC Merced, I earned my master degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Michigan State University.

Research Interests

My research broadly falls into general areas of high-performance system and architecture design(large-scale parallel/distributed systems). More specifically, I am interested in memory/storage/cache systems.
My most recent research work is focusing on exploring the efficient approach of utilizing emerging non-volatile memory (NVM) in HPC, cloud and database systems.
  • Parallel programming models and runtime
  • Performance optimization and modeling
